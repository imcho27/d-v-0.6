Writing Your Personal Statement

Your personal statement is an important part of your application. Here are some guidance points to help you get started:

  • Write in your own words. You do not need formal or legal language.
  • Describe the relationship and when things began to change.
  • Explain how the abuse affected you and, if relevant, your children.
  • Include dates and details where you feel safe and comfortable to do so.
  • Explain why the relationship has permanently broken down.
  • Mention any steps you have taken to leave or seek help.
  • You do not need to include every incident — focus on what feels most important to you.
  • A solicitor can help you review and refine your statement before submission.

Your statement will be read with care and sensitivity by the Home Office caseworker. Trauma and the impact of abuse are taken into account.

Local Support in Luton & Bedfordshire

The organisations below offer free, confidential support. You do not need to be ready to leave or take any action to contact them. They are here to help.

National Domestic Abuse Helpline

Free, confidential helpline available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Phone: 0808 2000 247

Run by: Refuge

If you are in immediate danger, please call 999.

Women's Aid Luton

Local support for women and children experiencing domestic abuse in Luton.

Offers refuge, outreach, and independent domestic violence advocates (IDVA).

Contact through the national Women's Aid website: womensaid.org.uk

Luton All Women's Centre

A community centre in Luton providing advice, advocacy, and support to women from all backgrounds, including those with immigration concerns.

Supports women regardless of immigration status or nationality.

Victim Support Luton

Free and confidential support for people affected by crime, including domestic abuse.

National Supportline: 08 08 16 89 111

Available 24/7. You do not have to report the crime to the police to access their support.
